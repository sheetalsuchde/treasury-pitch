Hi Sheetal — your loan is currently in default, and I want to help you get out of it. Being in default has real consequences: the Dept. of Education can seize your federal tax refund, garnish up to 15% of your Social Security benefits, order wage garnishment, and it causes serious long-term damage to your credit score — affecting your ability to rent, borrow, or qualify for housing.

Here are the five ways to resolve a defaulted federal student loan. I can help you with one of them:

Administrative loan discharge
Your loan may be cancelled if you qualify — for example, due to total and permanent disability, school closure, or identity theft fraud. Requires an application and review.

Request for review
If you believe the default was placed in error, you can formally object or dispute it. This puts collections on hold while the review is pending.

Loan consolidation
Combine your defaulted loan into a new Direct Consolidation Loan. Resolves default quickly, but the default notation remains on your credit report.

Pay off the debt
Pay the full balance in a lump sum to exit default immediately. Fastest resolution, but the default notation may remain on your credit report for up to 7 years.
